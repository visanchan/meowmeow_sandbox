# Pilot Rules

## Acceptance criteria for pilot applicants

A "yes" needs all of these:

- [ ] Brand sells cat products (food, toys, accessories, apparel, services)
- [ ] At least one upcoming booth/event in the next 90 days
- [ ] 10–100 active SKUs
- [ ] Accepts PromptPay or bank transfer (cash-only is OK too)
- [ ] Has a smartphone or tablet at the booth
- [ ] Owner can spend 30 min on a phone call once a week during the pilot

A "no" (or "later") if any of these:

- [ ] Not in the cat niche
- [ ] More than 200 SKUs (would need bulk-import polish first)
- [ ] Multi-branch retail or franchise model
- [ ] Needs official Thai tax invoice on day one
- [ ] Needs a receipt printer / barcode scanner on day one
- [ ] Asking for many custom features in the first conversation

## Pilot client commitments

Each accepted client agrees to:

1. Use the system at one full event during the pilot window.
2. Reply to a weekly check-in (5 min written or 30 min call).
3. Allow the founder to view their data for support purposes.
4. Report bugs within 24 hours of finding them.
5. Not share their invite code with anyone.

## Founder commitments

In exchange:

1. Free use of the system during the pilot.
2. Founder available on Line/WhatsApp during their event day.
3. Same-day fix for any blocking bug.
4. Optional: founder visits one event in person.

## Pilot exit

After the pilot:

- Client keeps all their data (export available any time).
- Founder may end the pilot if engagement is too low (no event in 60 days).
- Client may end the pilot at any time.
- Pricing, if introduced, is communicated 30 days before billing starts.

## What the pilot proves

We are explicitly trying to learn:

1. Does the application → approval → invite-code flow filter clients well?
2. Is the product setup step (cards + images) fast enough on a phone? Target: 30 SKUs in <30 min.
3. Does a real-DB POS feel as fast as the localStorage meowmeow on a booth tablet over 4G?
4. How often do staff need correction/void? Sample size 5 brands × 1 event each.
5. Are the dashboard + end-of-day reports good enough to replace manual cash-drawer counting?

If any answer is "no," we redesign that area before opening pilot to brand #6.
